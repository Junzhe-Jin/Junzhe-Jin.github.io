BIOral: Modular Intraoral Interfaces

Continuous Saliva Biomarker Sensing and On-Demand Drug Delivery.

DOI for our CHI EA (first vision) Video

Research Value

BIOral expands the boundaries of intraoral interfaces in HCI from purely mechanical inputs to advanced bio-interactive systems. By enabling hands-free, eyes-free continuous health monitoring and programmable on-demand drug delivery, this research demonstrates how miniaturized wearable technology can be seamlessly integrated into the human body. It provides a novel methodology for researchers and clinicians to design inclusive, health-oriented interactive systems that improve personal well-being without increasing user cognitive load.

Background & Motivation

Saliva contains crucial physiological biomarkers, yet continuous and unobtrusive monitoring remains a challenge in everyday settings. In the field of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), existing intraoral interfaces primarily focus on physical interactions—such as tongue gestures or teeth biting—rather than biochemical sensing. Monitoring oral health typically requires bulky equipment or frequent clinical visits. We designed BIOral to bridge this gap, aiming to create an unobtrusive, wearable platform inside the mouth that seamlessly integrates health monitoring into users' daily lives.

System Architecture & Implementation

The hardware relies on a custom SLA 3D-printed dental retainer made from biocompatible resin, integrating a digital color sensor (TCS34725) securely encapsulated using food-grade epoxy. To ensure safety and adaptability, we developed CapCell, a modular capsule system with a threaded locking mechanism, allowing users to easily swap sensing or drug-release modules.

For pH sensing, we fabricated edible hydrogels using anthocyanin and κ-carrageenan, whose colorimetric shifts are captured by the embedded RGB sensor. We implemented a robust signal-processing algorithm that normalizes the RGB readings to provide highly accurate pH estimates (ranging from pH 2 to 10). Furthermore, by parametrically tuning the geometric aperture of the CapCell, we engineered vitamin-infused hydrogels for programmable, sustained intraoral drug diffusion.
