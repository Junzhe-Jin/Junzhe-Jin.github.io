About

I am interested in Human-Computer Interaction, with a focus on Embodied Haptics and Haptics of Things that transform wearable devices and everyday objects into interactive tactile displays. My work explores the development of body-anchored systems using vibration and electrovibration to enable intuitive, non-visual interaction within ubiquitous computing environments[cite: 15]. By leveraging my research experience, I aim to design haptic feedback mechanisms that integrate seamlessly into clothing and physical artifacts to support task performance under realistic constraints like mobility and limited attention[cite: 16]. More broadly, I seek to advance human-centered wearable systems that balance high-fidelity tactile feedback with long-term comfort and empirical usability[cite: 17].
