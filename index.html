Hello, I'm Junzhe JIN

HCI Researcher & Master's Student at Aarhus University

I am an HCI researcher mainly focusing on Embodied Interaction and Tangible User Interfaces (TUIs). My research explores the development of shape-changing interfaces and soft robotics, investigating how the physical transformation and spatial movement of non-anthropomorphic robots can facilitate intuitive human-robot communication to create more inclusive assistive technologies and interactive systems.

Master's Research Projects

Dynamic Crossmodal Mappings in Shape-Changing Interfaces

Jan 2026 - Present | Master Thesis Intern, University of Bristol

Designed and fabricated a pneumatically actuated soft shape-changing device. Conducted a systematic user study to investigate how dynamic shape-change influences crossmodal mappings to visual color dimensions, aiming to derive predictive design principles for affect-driven and inclusive interfaces.

SmartPrompt: Interactive AI-Assisted System

Jan 2025 - May 2025 | Key Member, Aarhus University

Designed an interactive Human-AI co-creation system bridging the gap between user intent and LLM execution. Utilized explainable radar charts and real-time NLP to detect linguistic ambiguities, reducing users' cognitive load during iterative prompt engineering.

BIOral: Modular Intraoral Interfaces

Oct 2024 - Apr 2025 | Research Intern, Aarhus University

Developed BIOral, a modular intraoral bio-interactive interface enabling continuous saliva biomarker sensing and on-demand drug delivery. Combined 3D printed hardware with colorimetric signal processing to achieve accurate pH estimation and programmable drug diffusion.

VisionNav: Vibration-Guided IoT Navigation Companion

Oct 2024 - Dec 2024 | Key Member, Aarhus University

Engineered a non-visual, haptic-based IoT navigation assistant for visually impaired users or individuals in noisy environments. Integrated smartphone computer vision, MQTT messaging, and dual haptic actuators to generate real-time tactile navigation feedback.
